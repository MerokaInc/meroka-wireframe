The 11 Legal Data Fields
Practice, Employer, and System fields powering the legal agent
▼
Practice Side (P1 – P4)
P1 Legal Entity Name + NPI
Source: CMS PECOS
Public Easy Required
DescriptionOfficial legal entity name and National Provider Identifier, used as the primary key for practice identity and regulatory lookups.
Data FormatString (entity name) + 10-digit NPI number
Update Freq.Monthly refresh from PECOS API
click to expand
P2 Active MA Medical Licence (Y/N)
Source: MA Board of Registration in Medicine
Public Hard Gate Check
DescriptionBoolean check confirming at least one physician at the practice holds an active, unrestricted Massachusetts medical licence. Blocks onboarding if false.
Data FormatBoolean (Y/N) + licence number + expiry date
Update Freq.Weekly scrape; real-time check at onboarding
click to expand
P3 OIG Exclusion Status (Y/N)
Source: OIG LEIE Database
Public Easy Gate Check
DescriptionBoolean check against the OIG List of Excluded Individuals/Entities. Any exclusion flags human review and blocks automated onboarding.
Data FormatBoolean (Y/N) + exclusion type if applicable
Update Freq.Monthly file from OIG; real-time API at onboarding
click to expand
P4 Price List (Procedure + Price + Capacity)
Source: Practice self-submitted
Private Required
DescriptionPractice-submitted schedule of procedures, pricing, and available capacity. Forms the basis for Schedule B of the T&Cs and is the core marketplace matching data.
Data FormatJSON array: [{procedure, cpt_code, price_cents, weekly_slots}]
Update Freq.Practice-initiated; refreshed on contract renewal
click to expand
Employer Side (E1 – E3)
E1 Legal Entity Name + FEIN
Source: Form 5500 / Self-reported
Public Required
DescriptionOfficial legal entity name and Federal Employer Identification Number. Cross-referenced with Form 5500 filings where available to confirm entity identity.
Data FormatString (entity name) + 9-digit FEIN (XX-XXXXXXX)
Update Freq.At onboarding; annual refresh with Form 5500
click to expand
E2 Plan Type: Self-Funded (Y/N)
Source: Form 5500 Schedule A
Public Required
DescriptionBoolean confirming the employer's health plan is self-funded (not fully insured). Critical for ERISA preemption analysis and contract structure.
Data FormatBoolean (Y/N) + plan type details
Update Freq.Annual via Form 5500; confirmed at onboarding
click to expand
E3 Employer State
Source: Self-reported
Private Required
DescriptionState of incorporation or primary operations for the employer. Used to determine applicable state regulations, compliance flags, and contract template variant.
Data Format2-letter state code (e.g., MA, NY)
Update Freq.At onboarding; updated if employer relocates
click to expand
System Fields (S1 – S4)
S1 MA Template Variant
Source: Foley (quarterly updates)
Internal Required
DescriptionMassachusetts-specific contract template variant maintained by Foley & Lardner. Contains state-compliant language for DPC agreements, ERISA carve-outs, and BAA provisions.
Data FormatTemplate ID + version string + effective date
Update Freq.Quarterly review by Foley; ad-hoc for regulatory changes
click to expand
S2 State Compliance Flags
Source: MA Regulatory Matrix
Internal Required
DescriptionDerived compliance flags from the state regulatory matrix. Determines which contract clauses, disclosures, and addenda are required for a given state.
Data FormatJSON object: {dpc_statute: bool, tpa_exempt: bool, baa_required: bool, ...}
Update Freq.Updated when regulatory matrix changes; quarterly review
click to expand
S3 TPA Requirements
Source: Foley confirmation
Internal Pending Foley
DescriptionThird-Party Administrator requirements and classification guidance. Determines whether Meroka's platform fee structure requires TPA licensing in given states.
Data FormatStructured guidance document + boolean flags per state
Update Freq.Pending initial Foley determination; then as-needed
click to expand
S4 Contract Template ID
Source: Generated by system
Internal Required
DescriptionSystem-generated unique identifier linking a specific contract to its template version, state variant, and populated data fields. Used for audit trail and version control.
Data FormatUUID v4 + template version hash
Update Freq.Generated at contract creation; immutable once signed

Appendix A
Matched Practices
Payment Flow
Employer → Practice   Direct payment for services
Meroka → Platform Fee   Separate billing (not intermediary)
Principle 1
No Intermediary Risk
Meroka never touches payment between employer and practice. Platform fee is separate.
Principle 2
ERISA Shield
Self-funded employer plans benefit from federal ERISA preemption of state insurance regs.
Principle 3
DPC Safe Harbor
MGL c.176Y explicitly exempts DPC membership fees from insurance classification.
Principle 4
Data Minimization
Only 11 data fields collected. PHI handled under BAA. No unnecessary data retention.

Future State
Advanced regulatory domains activated by growth triggers
▼
Anti-Kickback Statute (AKS)
Federal AKS analysis becomes relevant when the marketplace handles any federal healthcare program patients or when payment flows could be construed as inducements for referrals.
Trigger: Platform begins handling Medicare/Medicaid patients, or payment structures include referral-based incentives.
Stark Law (Physician Self-Referral)
Stark Law compliance required when physicians have financial relationships with entities they refer to. Relevant if practice ownership structures create referral loops within the marketplace.
Trigger: Physician ownership or investment interest in marketplace practices creates potential self-referral patterns.
Clinically Integrated Networks (CIN)
CIN monitoring and antitrust analysis required when marketplace concentration could enable coordinated pricing or reduce competition in local markets.
Trigger: Marketplace reaches sufficient practice density in a geography to warrant FTC/DOJ antitrust review.
